my thoughts...

the best music my ears have heard
if i had not found Aphex Twin's music
i think i wouldn't be in the same place
i am at today

his stunningly beautiful selected ambient works 85-92 is an amazing tone setter for his entire future discography
the beautiful sampling of the track 'xtal', deep reverb that makes a truly fucking unreal atmopshere at 'tha'
and my most favourite track 'hedphelym', that evokes such a incontrollably strong feeling of kenopsia are all masterpieces by the young aphex twin.
the whining synths of 'hedphelym' and the banging distorted hardcore kicks almost don't go along, but they weirdly do.
the feeling of "kenopsia" is evoked by mere thought of the track, banging music in a big club, but its totally empty, no one to be seen, anywhere.

i could go in more detail about every album but in my personal opinion, aphex twin deserves a whole page for himself as to how much influence he has given and how much his music truly healed me in turbulent moments of my early and current life.


thank you.

my thoughts...

boards of canada are the other artists that deserve a whole fucking page on my site as to how unbelievably beautiful all of their music is.

music has the right to children, their most popular work, leaves the listener absolutely stoked at the end, battling with the overwhelming feeling of nostaliga they have expirienced by mere sound waves and their brain comprehending them.
many elements of nature and a child's magical imagination in a sound form give an overbearing emotional impact to the listener.

geogaddi, noticeably darker in tone than the last album, sometimes metaphorically makes the listener float, sometimes makes them uncomfortable, sometimes even frightened.
the interludes are short, but absolutely packed with ear candy and atmospheric landscapes.

as with aphex twin i could go in inexplainable amounts of detail into each track, and each album of the duo.
but i have to mention 'a few old tunes volume 2'.
music has the right to children is child's play compared to a few old tunes volume 2. every track makes a new memory inside the listeners head, recalls memories, and with the memories in thought, the ongoing music synergizes with the overwhelming amount of memories making an out of earth experience.
heck this album only can get a whole dedicated page about it. every fucking album can, 'twoism' especially.

thank you.
